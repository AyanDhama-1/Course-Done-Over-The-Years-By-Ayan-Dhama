RED FORT
The Red Fort, located in Delhi, India, is a historic fortification that served as the main residence of the Mughal emperors for nearly 200 years. Built in 1639 by Emperor Shah Jahan, the fort is named for its massive enclosing walls of red sandstone.

It is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a symbol of India's rich cultural heritage. The Red Fort is known for its stunning architecture, including the Diwan-i-Aam, Diwan-i-Khas, and the beautiful gardens within its premises.
